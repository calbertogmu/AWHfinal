American Women and The Holocaust

This project came about through somewhat accidental means. When researching oral histories of Holocaust survivors, I stumbled across the oral history interview of Pat Lynch. When I listened to her interview, I realized how important the accounts of liberators are. I began digging and found less than twenty interviews of U.S. Army nurses that cared for the survivors of NAZI concentration camps.

It is not the place of the website author at this time to analyze these oral histories. The information created by the website author is limited to the summaries under select oral histories. In an effort to accomodate the members of the deaf and hard of hearing communities, the website author has used AdobePremierPro to add captions to the video recorded oral histories. Some have been checked for typos, but there are some that have only the captions automatically created by the program.

The website uses a template created by TooPlate, and was edited using AdobeDreamweaver.

All digital material located within the website was procured from the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum, and are listed in no particular order.

This website, and its author are NOT affilliated with the United States Holocaust Memorial Musuem; this website is for educational purposes only.

The material contained within this website is sensitive, and may not be appropriate for all audiences. Viewer discretion advised.  

Video Oral Histories

Pat Lynch

Oral History Interview of Pat Lynch. In this interview, lynch describes her experience working as a nurse at a field hospital in Germany after the liberation of a concentration camp. It has been subtititled by the website creator for the Deaf/HOH community.

Ethel Stafford

Ethel Stafford tells the story of her time as a US Army nurse. She was with the division that took over the town of Gardelegen, Germany. She describes her weariness as she enters the town where the Nazis trapped 1,016 prisoners in a barn and burned them alive. This atrocity has come to be known as the Gardelegen massacre. The video was subtitled by the website creator for the Deaf/HOH, it was done using Adobe Audition, and I have not edited it for typos.

Marjorie Butterfield

The video was subtitled by the website creator for the Deaf/HOH, it was done using Adobe Audition, and I have not edited it for typos.

Marie Ellifritz


Marie Ellifritz was a US Army nurse with the 130th Evacuation hospital, who took part in the liberation of Mathausen concentration camp. She speaks of having her religion knocked out from under feet. The video was subtitled by the website creator for the Deaf/HOH, it was done using Adobe Audition, and I have not edited it for typos.
